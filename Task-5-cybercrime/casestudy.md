
# Case Study: Phishing Attack

**Overview:**
Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords, bank details, or personal identification, by pretending to be a trustworthy entity. It is one of the most common forms of online fraud.

**How it Happens (Step-by-Step):**

1. The attacker sends a fake email, SMS, or social media message that appears to come from a legitimate source, such as a bank, e-commerce site, or social network.
2. The message contains a sense of urgency, such as “Your account will be suspended” or “Verify your payment immediately.”
3. The victim is prompted to click on a malicious link that leads to a counterfeit website resembling the official one.
4. The victim enters sensitive information, which is captured by the attacker.
5. The attacker then uses the stolen data for unauthorized access, financial theft, or identity fraud.

**Typical Targets:**
Phishing attacks target anyone who uses digital services, but college students and young adults are particularly vulnerable because of frequent online transactions, social media usage, and less awareness of cybersecurity risks.

**Consequences:**
Victims may lose money, have their accounts compromised, or suffer identity theft. In addition, phishing can damage personal and professional reputation and create long-term financial and emotional stress. Organizations targeted by phishing may also face data breaches, legal issues, and loss of customer trust.

**Reference (Example):**
A well-known incident involved students receiving emails mimicking their college’s finance office, resulting in multiple unauthorized transactions and stolen credentials.
